After graduating Magna Cum Laude with a BBA in Management, I worked in the healthcare system for seven years. Here I gained and exercised many dynamic skills such as communication, teamwork, problem solving, and customer service.

Ready for a change of pace, I turned my interest to a new field of study. I enrolled in UT Austin's Full Stack Coding Bootcamp for a 12-week course starting in September. This was the most challenging yet rewarding 3 months of my life. During this time, I continued to raise my 1 year old daughter, and gave birth to our second child. I have to give a TON of credit to my husband and mother for helping so much with the kids while I studdied. I felt like the typical coding student being flooded with information and loving (just about) every minute of it. Throughout the course, I frequently referenced a quote hanging on my wall that read "Every challenge is an opportunity to learn something new or better". These words helped to give me the strength to push forward through any difficult times. After a December graduation, I was ready to begin my career utilizing the skills I have devoted much time and effort to.

I was fortunate to find an internship opportunity soon after graduation with a company whose values are closely aligned with my own. Awarded a spot on an international team, I was given the chance to help convert a computer application from the 1990's to a modernized web application using Angular and ASP.NET in C#. Throughout this internship, I not only learned many valuable coding skills, but I also was fortuate enough to witness the value in a successful team. Each member brings a unique view to the meeting; this is what helps to make our application the best it can be, one step at a time.

Overall, I am thrilled with my first two coding experiences and looking forward to what this field holds for me in the future.
